You're Not Imagining It.

Welcome to Spectrum Kids Hub — a place built for families like yours. Whatever brought you here, you're in exactly the right place.

Explore the guides Join the community

Maybe it started with a comment from a teacher. Or a birthday party where things just felt... harder than they should. Maybe you've been quietly Googling at midnight, typing words you're not quite ready to say out loud yet.

If any of that sounds familiar, you're in exactly the right place.

That quiet hunch you've been carrying? It deserves to be taken seriously. And so do you.

Perhaps you're at the very beginning — just starting to wonder, still not sure if you're overreacting, looking for reassurance or a nudge in the right direction. We get it. That stage is exhausting in its own particular way.

Or maybe you've been deep in it for years. You've done the CAMHS waiting lists, the school SENCO meetings, the stack of reports on the shelf. You know your child inside out and you're still searching for that one piece of information that clicks everything into place. We see you too.

Spectrum Kids Hub doesn't ask you to be at any particular stage. Whether neurodiversity is a brand new word in your world or you could deliver a TED talk on sensory processing, there's something here for you.

Introducing Nutricore Health

Nourishing the whole family

There's a growing body of evidence suggesting that diet, gut health, sleep, movement, and overall lifestyle can play a meaningful role in how neurodiverse children and adults feel day to day.

Not as a cure — there is no cure, and we'd never suggest there should be — but as one part of a bigger picture of wellbeing. Nutricore Health is our dedicated space for this, covering everything from nutrient-rich eating and reducing ultra-processed foods to sleep hygiene, physical activity, and managing stress as a family.

Everything here is educational and informational, written with care and reviewed for accuracy. We're not here to prescribe, replace your GP, or sell you anything. We're here to share what the research says and help you make informed choices that work for your family.

Sometimes small, sustainable changes in how a family eats, sleeps, and moves can make the difficult days feel just a little more manageable.

A note from the person behind this

I want to be honest with you about what Spectrum Kids Hub is — and what it isn't.

I'm a parent at the beginning of my own journey, just like many of you reading this. I started building this site partly to make sense of everything I was learning, partly because pulling information together is how I process things, and partly because — if I'm being completely honest — it makes the whole thing feel less overwhelming. Building something useful is how I cope. It turns out, learning by doing is very much my thing.

I'm also a nutritional therapist and health coach, so the diet and lifestyle content you'll find here comes from a place of genuine professional knowledge as well as personal experience. But everything else — the assessment guides, the education stuff, the emotional bits — that's me learning alongside you, gathering the best information I can find, and sharing it as I go.

This site grows as my journey does. It's a living thing — not a finished product.

That means it won't always be perfect. Some things might be out of date. Some things might be incomplete. Some areas will be thin right now and richer in a few months' time. I'd rather share something real and growing than wait until it's polished.

This is a community project — in every sense. If you know of a resource, a topic, or a story that belongs here, please share it. If you spot something that's out of date, or not quite right, please tell me. There's no ego here — just a genuine desire to get this right and keep it useful.

Spectrum Kids Hub isn't a service I'm selling. There's nothing to buy. It's a peer-to-peer space — me sharing what I'm learning, others sharing what they know, and all of us building something better together than any of us could manage alone.

If you have something to add — a resource, a correction, a story, an idea — reach out. This is yours as much as it is mine.

Thank you for being here. I genuinely mean that.
